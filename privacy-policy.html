Face Data Collection and Usage for Identity Verification

In our commitment to uphold the highest standards of user privacy and data security, we meticulously delineate our practices regarding the collection, use, disclosure, and retention of facial data obtained for identity verification purposes. Our application employs Amazon Web Services (AWS) Recognition for the sole purpose of identity verification, a process which necessitates a photograph of the user's face. Please be assured that this process is designed to respect user privacy in the following manner:
‍
Data Collection: Our application does not collect or store face data within its own systems. When a user submits a photo for identity verification, this photo is transmitted directly to AWS Recognition without any duplication or retention on our end.

Data Usage: The photo provided by the user is exclusively used for the intended function of verifying the identity of the individual. We do not exploit this photo for any ancillary purposes, nor do we extract any additional data from this process.

Data Disclosure: There is a firm policy against sharing the submitted photo or any facial data derived from it with third parties. The integrity of our users' personal data is paramount, and as such, we ensure there are stringent controls against unauthorized disclosure.

Data Retention: We adhere to a strict policy of not retaining face data after the verification process. Once the photograph is forwarded to AWS Recognition and the necessary verification is complete, we ensure that the photo is not saved, stored, or otherwise retained in any of our systems or on any external storage devices.

Data Security: We employ robust security measures to safeguard the transmission of the photo to AWS Recognition, ensuring the data is encrypted in transit and handled with the utmost care.

Definitions of terms

This section provides you with an overview of the terms used in this privacy policy. Many of the terms are taken from the law and defined primarily in Art. 4 of the GDPR. The legal definitions are binding. The following explanations, on the other hand, are primarily intended to aid understanding. The terms are sorted alphabetically.
- Personal data: "Personal data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person (hereinafter "data subject"); an identifiable natural person is one who can be identified, directly or indirectly, in particular by reference to an identifier such as a name, an identification number, location data, an online identifier (e.g. cookie) or to one or more factors specific to the physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural or social identity of that natural person.
- Controller: "Controller" means the natural or legal person, public authority, agency or other body which alone or jointly with others determines the purposes and means of the processing of personal data.
- Processing: "Processing" means any operation or set of operations which is performed upon personal data, whether or not by automatic means. The term is broad and includes virtually any handling of data, whether collecting, evaluating, storing, transmitting or erasing.
